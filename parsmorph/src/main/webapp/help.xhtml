INTRODUCTION
In this study we investigate the theoretical base of Persian morphology system and design a Persian morphological analyzer named “Pars Morph”. Pars Morph is a system based on the morphological rules of Persian language. It analyzes and parses the internal structure of Persian words regarding the inflection and word formation system of the language. It also determines the part of speech and the grammatical role of each compromising component. Pars morph uses a lexicon containing about 45000 words and is strictly defined in the framework of Persian morphological rules and a comprehensive linguistic research background. It can analyze complex words, inflectional forms and even out-of-vocabulary words. The accuracy of the first version of the software is about 95% which can be improved to near 100% by adding syntactic information and considering the problem of homographs and special features of the Persian language. It can also be used in pure linguistic research and automatic processing of Persian language. PERSIAN INFLECTION SYSTEM

The word formation system of Persian language is formalized in the design of Pars Morph. Two main forms of word formation in Persian language are derivation and compounding. Pars Morph refers to Flexicon and analyzes derived/compound words using the derivation and compounding capabilities. Derivation We provided Pars Morph with a list of derivational affixes and patterns of derivations in Persian language. In the patterns of derivations, it is specified which derivational affixes are added to which specific stems regarding their syntactic category; and the syntactic category of the resulting word will be identified. For example the word “دانشگاهی” is broken up into “دان” (v1)+ “ش” (N=اسم‌ساز)+ “گاه” (N=اسم‌ساز) + “ی” (A=صفت‌ساز). Using this information, Pars Morph can analyze out-of-vocabulary words and even can suggest potential analyses for the corresponding words. Compounding
Simple lexemes can be combined together and form compound lexemes. Often the components of a compound lexeme are themselves lexical entries in software’s lexicon. In this case the syntactic categories of combining components and the compound lexeme are given. Out-of-vocabulary compound words can also be analyzed using the main lexicon of the software and considering the combining patterns. It is worth mentioning that the software is not relied on spaces, i.e. if we write “آهنخوار” as “آهن خوار” with any combinations of spaces, the result is the same. In the case of compound-complex words, Pars Morph considers the rules of derivation and compounding simultaneously and breaks up the word into its constituents in which the syntactic category of each component is specified. For example words like “کارخانه‌چی” or “دانشجو” are analyzed as: “کار” (N1)+ “خانه” (N1)+ “چی” (N=تکواژ اشتقاقی اسم‌ساز), “دان” (V1)+ “ش” (N=تکواژ اشتقاقی اسم‌ساز) + “جو” (V1), respectively.
